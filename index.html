| Copyright & Accepting Cookies

Note: Putting a copyright notice on the bottom of a website or blog is a form of protection as it states that the material displayed is not to be used without the permission of the owner. In fact, you don't even need the notice to claim copyright as the law eliminated the requirement of public notice in 1989. As for Cookies, they're files downloaded and stored by your browser. The reason you are now asked to accept them when visiting a website is due to the new GDPR guidelines regarding full transparency of collected personal data and what it's been used for, as they have to obtain your permission to install cookies.
